CEC Content Engine

How to use this tool

This tool uses Claude AI to help you create content for CEC: newsletters, blog posts, LinkedIn posts and case studies. It knows CEC's tone of voice, services, case studies and commercial context. The more you put in, the better the output.

🎤 Tip: use voice to fill in the brief

The fastest way to use this tool is with a voice dictation tool. Speak your brief into the topic, theme and key points fields. WhisperFlow works well for this, but any voice-to-text tool will do. Instead of typing a structured brief, just talk through the project, the angle and what you want to say. The AI will structure it.

📅
Calendar

Your master content plan. See all 12 months at a glance. Click any month to view, add or manage content items. Each item tracks status from Idea through to Published. Content you create in the Studio saves here automatically.

✎
Content Studio

Where you write. Choose a format (Newsletter, Blog, LinkedIn), pick a story type, fill in the brief and click Generate. Refine with instructions. Then use the Spin Off bar to convert to other formats with one click.

📄
Case Studies

Fill in the project details using the form, or use Chat mode to let Claude interview you. Once generated, use the spin-off buttons to turn the case study into a blog post, LinkedIn post or newsletter feature.

🔁
Spin Off

Any piece of content can become another format. Generate a newsletter, then spin it off as a LinkedIn post. Build a case study, then spin it off as a blog. The AI carries the core story across and adapts the format, length and tone.

How the Content Studio works

1
Pick your story type

This tells Claude the narrative framework to use. A field encounter opens with the moment. A commercial risk piece leads with the client pain. A policy update states what changed first. Picking the right type shapes the whole piece.

2
Fill in the brief

Topic, theme and angle, key points to cover. You do not need to write perfectly here. Notes, bullet points, even rough voice dictation all work. The more context you give, the more the output will sound like it came from someone who knows CEC.

3
Generate and refine

Click Generate. Read the output. Use the Refine box to give a single instruction: "make it shorter", "lead with the outcome", "add a survey window reminder". Each refinement improves the version without starting over.

4
Copy and finish

The tool gets you to around 90% of the way there. Copy the output and do the final 10% in Word, your CMS or wherever it is going. That last pass is where you add the detail only you know, check facts, and make it fully yours.

Story types and when to use them

Field encounter

Something your ecologists witnessed in nature. Dormice in a box. A hedgehog self-anointing with toad venom. A barn owl at the end of a bat survey. Opens with the moment. Explains the science. Connects to why fieldwork matters.

Policy or legal update

BNG changes, new survey guidance, planning legislation. Lead with what changed. Explain what it means for your clients. Tell them what to do. No hedging.

Commercial risk

Targeting developers or landowners around cost, delay or planning risk. Problem, Agitate, Solution. Names the pain, makes it real, presents CEC as the practical answer.

Project story

A case study told as narrative. Challenge, what CEC did, outcome. Specific and concrete throughout. Best when you have a clear outcome to anchor the story.

Celebration or community

Awards, team news, wildlife milestones, CWT moments. Warmth first. No hard sell. Just share what happened and invite the reader in.

How the tool knows about CEC

The tool has a built-in system prompt that tells Claude everything it needs to write in CEC's voice. This includes:

✓

CEC's tone of voice rules, including no em dashes, field voice, local specificity and no hyperbole

✓

Key themes: survey windows, BNG, planning risk, single point of contact, Cornwall Wildlife Trust ownership

✓

Case studies: Newquay Cycle Hub, Langarth Garden Village, A30, Greystones, Making Space for Nature and more

✓

The risk framework: what happens when developers get ecology wrong and how CEC solves it

✓

Story type frameworks: the narrative structure that matches each type of content

As CEC adds new case studies and projects, these can be added to the tool so it stays current.

A note on the output

This tool is designed to get you to 90%. The final 10% is yours: check the facts, add the detail only you know, adjust any phrasing that does not feel right. The best results come when you treat the output as a strong first draft, not a finished piece.
